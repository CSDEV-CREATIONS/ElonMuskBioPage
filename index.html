About Elon Musk

  • 1971: Elon was born on June 28, 1971 in Petoria, South Africa where he grew up as a child
  • 1995: He moved to California to attend Stanford University but decided to instead pursue a business career, co-founding the web software company Zip2 with his brother Kimbal
  • 1999: Musk co-founded online bank X.com, which would later merge with Confinity in 2000 to form PayPal. The company was then bought by eBay in 2002 for $1.5 billion.
  • 2002: Musk co-founded SpaceX which was at the time an aerospace manufacturer and space transport services company, which Elon Musk serves as CEO and Chief Engineer.
  • 2004: In 2004, Elon became an early investor in the electric vehicle manufacturer Tesla Motors, Inc. (Now Tesla, Inc.). He became the company's chairman and product architect, eventually assuming the position of CEO in 2008
  • 2006: He helped create SolarCity, which was a solar energy company that had been later acquired by Tesla and would then become Tesla Energy
  • 2015: Elon had co-founded OpenAI, a nonprofit research company promoting friendly artificial intelligence (AI)
  • 2016: He co-founded Neuralink, a neurotechnology company focused on developing brain-computer interfaces
  • 2017: He founded The Boring Company to construct tunnels. Early that year, the company had begun discussions with regulatory bodies and had initiated construction of a 30-foot (9.1 m) wide, 50-foot (15 m) long, and 15-foot (4.6 m) deep "test trench" on the premises of SpaceX's offices as it required no permits.
  • 2022: On April 4, 2022 Musk agreed to a deal that would appoint him to Twitter's board of directors and prohibit him from acquiring more than 14.9% of the company. But then on April 13, 2022 Musk had made a $43 billion offer to by Twitter, launching a takeover bid to buy 100% of Twitter's stock at $54.20 per share. Exactly a month after announcing the takeover, Musk stated that the deal was "on hold" following a report that 5 percent of twitter's daily active users were spam accounts, causing twitter shares to drop more than 10 percent. Now Although Musk initially clarified that he remained committed to the acauisition, he later had sent a notification of his termination of the deal in July. Twitter is now suing Elon Musk for breaching a legally binding agreement to purchase Twitter
